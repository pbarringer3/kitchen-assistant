# Mexican-Style Stuffed Portobello Caps

**Eat to Live Compliant** | Serves: 2–3 | Prep: 15 min | Cook: 10–15 min

## Ingredients

### Mushrooms & Marinade
- 4 large portobello mushroom caps, stems removed
- 3 tbsp fresh lime juice (about 2 limes)
- 1 tbsp coconut aminos or low-sodium soy sauce
- 2 cloves garlic, minced
- 1 tsp cumin
- 1 tsp chili powder
- ½ tsp smoked paprika
- ½ tsp onion powder
- Black pepper to taste

### Filling
- 1 can (15 oz) black beans, drained and rinsed
- 1 cup corn (fresh, frozen/thawed, or fire-roasted)
- 1 cup cherry tomatoes, quartered
- ½ red onion, finely diced
- 1 red bell pepper, diced small
- 1 jalapeño, seeded and minced (optional)
- ¼ cup fresh cilantro, chopped
- Juice of 1 lime
- ½ tsp cumin
- ½ tsp chili powder
- Black pepper to taste

### Mango Avocado Salsa
- 1 ripe mango, diced small
- 1 ripe avocado, diced
- ¼ red onion, finely diced
- 1 jalapeño, seeded and minced
- 2 tbsp fresh cilantro, chopped
- Juice of 1 lime
- Pinch of black pepper

### Optional Garnishes
- Shredded romaine or cabbage
- Extra lime wedges
- Hot sauce (no-oil, low-sodium)
- Cashew sour cream (see below)

## Instructions

**Step 1 — Make the Salsa First**
Combine mango, avocado, red onion, jalapeño, cilantro, and lime juice in a bowl. Toss gently so avocado doesn't mash. Cover and refrigerate until ready to serve.

**Step 2 — Marinate the Mushrooms**
Clean caps with a damp paper towel. Scrape out gills with a spoon if desired. Whisk together marinade ingredients and brush generously over both sides. Let sit 15–30 minutes.

**Step 3 — Make the Filling**
In a skillet over medium heat with a splash of water or broth, sauté red onion and bell pepper 4–5 minutes. Add garlic, cumin, chili powder, and jalapeño; stir 1 minute. Add black beans and corn; cook 3–4 minutes. Remove from heat and stir in cherry tomatoes, cilantro, and lime juice. Season with black pepper.

**Step 4 — Cook the Mushrooms**

*Air Fry (preferred):*
Preheat to 375°F. Place caps gill-side up without overlapping. Cook 8–10 minutes until tender with slightly crispy edges.

*Broil:*
Preheat broiler to high. Place caps gill-side down 4–5 minutes, then flip gill-side up and broil another 4–5 minutes until caramelized and tender.

**Step 5 — Assemble**
Place cooked caps gill-side up. Spoon warm filling into each cap. Top with mango avocado salsa, garnishes, and serve immediately.

## Optional Cashew Sour Cream
*(Small amounts are ETL-approved)*
- ½ cup raw cashews, soaked 2–4 hours and drained
- Juice of 1 lemon
- 1 tsp apple cider vinegar
- Pinch of salt
- 3–4 tbsp water to blend

Blend until completely smooth. Drizzle over caps before serving.
